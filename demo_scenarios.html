🎓 Anticipated Professor Questions

Q: "Why not use Deep RL like PPO?"
PPO requires 97,000 episodes and GPU resources (Samsung Research 2024). Our Approximate Q-Learning with 12 features achieves generalization in seconds, making it practical for mobile deployment. This is a deliberate engineering trade-off.
Q: "How do you handle the cold-start problem?"
Two strategies: (1) Optimistic Initialization — Q₀(s,a) = Vmax = 5.0, which forces exploration of unvisited states. (2) Synthetic pre-training with 3,000 simulated episodes before deployment. This mirrors the approach validated in the Apptivate study.
Q: "Why does weight take so long to change?"
By design. We implemented the Accumulator Pattern: weight/muscle/fitness use fractional accumulators that only trigger a level change when |acc| ≥ 1.0. This prevents the physiologically impossible scenario of losing 3 weight levels in 5 days.
Q: "43,750 states — isn't that too many for tabular Q-Learning?"
Yes, and that's exactly why we also implemented Approximate Q-Learning. With only 12 weighted features, it generalizes across all states without visiting each one. The comparison between both approaches on the same environment is one of our key contributions.
Q: "What's the difference between Value Iteration and Q-Learning?"
Value Iteration is model-based — it requires complete knowledge of transition probabilities T(s,a,s') and computes the exact optimal policy. Q-Learning is model-free — it learns from trial-and-error experience without knowing environment dynamics. When all 3 algorithms agree on the same recommendation, it validates convergence.
Q: "Why did you include A* Search in an RL project?"
A* serves a fundamentally different purpose: multi-day planning. While Q-Learning answers "what should I do today?", A* Search answers "what's the optimal 14-day workout plan to reach my goal?". It complements RL by providing interpretable, step-by-step plans the user can follow.
